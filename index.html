Planning tools vs. diagnostic tools

Arc-itect has two kinds of tools, and knowing the difference matters.

Planning tools (the Planning tab) are for before and during writing. Brainstorm your beats. Work out your premise. Dump scene ideas. Track questions you need to answer. These are freeform and don't impose structure on you.

Diagnostic tools (Plot and Arcs tabs) are frameworks like Save the Cat, the Hero's Journey, and Story Circle. These weren't invented as writing instructions. They're reverse-engineered from stories that worked. Campbell didn't create the Hero's Journey; he noticed it kept appearing in myths. Snyder didn't invent Save the Cat; he analyzed why certain films succeeded.

The traditional use for diagnostic tools: write your story first, feel dissatisfied or sense a problem you can't identify, then apply the framework to diagnose what's off. "My second act drags?" Check your midpoint. "The ending feels unearned?" Examine your character's arc beats.

You can use diagnostic frameworks for planning. But if you do, write your story ideas first, then see which beats they naturally fit into. Don't start with an empty "Midpoint" box and try to invent something to fill it. That approach produces stories that hit all the marks but feel hollow because the beats exist to satisfy the framework rather than to serve your actual story.
